Yucheng Jin 金昱成


Yucheng Jin is an incoming Assistant Professor in Computer Science at Duke Kunshan University. He is also a member of Positive Intelligence Lab. Prior to his current role, he was a Research Assistant Professor at Hong Kong Baptist University, where he contributed significantly to the field of Human-computer Interaction. His industry credentials are equally impressive, having spent four years as a senior UX designer at Lenovo Research. His academic journey culminated in a Ph.D. in Augment Group from the University of Leuven (KU Leuven). He has co-authored over 30 papers for premier conferences and journals in Human-Computer Interaction, such as CHI, CSCW, and IJHCS. His innovative work has also led to the issuance of more than ten patents. Furthermore, he regularly serves as a program committee member for esteemed ACM conferences like RecSys and IUI.

Research Interests

His research is situated in the long-standing quest to "Augmenting human intellect." He studies how humans interact / collaborate with AI systems in two major application domains: mental well-being and augmented creativity. His research interests include the following topics:

  • Human-AI Interaction and Collaboration
  • UX of Recommender Systems (e.g., trust, explanation, personalization, and user control)
  • Music and Augmented Creativity
  • Mental Health and Well-being
